How to Explain This Project

Whether it's a viva, interview, or presentation — use these structured talking points to walk anyone through your project confidently.

01 Opening Introduction (30 seconds) +
Say this:

"This project is about building a Machine Learning model that forecasts future sales or demand for a business, based on its historical data. The core problem is — businesses often don't know how much product to stock or how much revenue to expect next month. My model solves this by learning patterns from past data and projecting into the future."

Key words to use: time-series, historical patterns, regression, prediction horizon, business value.
02 Data & Preprocessing (Technical) +
Explain like this:
  • Dataset: "I used a historical sales dataset with monthly/weekly records including date, units sold, price, and category."
  • Cleaning: "I handled missing values using forward-fill for time-series continuity, removed outliers using IQR, and converted dates to proper datetime format."
  • Feature Engineering: "I extracted lag features (sales from previous 1, 3, 6 months), rolling averages, month/quarter indicators for seasonality, and a linear time index for trend capture."

Key code concepts: df['lag_1'] = df['sales'].shift(1), df.rolling(3).mean(), pd.to_datetime()
03 Model Selection & Why +
Walk through your model choice:
  • ARIMA/SARIMA: "For stationary data with clear trend. I used ADF test to check stationarity and ACF/PACF plots to set p, d, q parameters."
  • Exponential Smoothing (ETS): "Assigns exponentially decreasing weights to past observations — recent data matters more. Great for smooth, stable sales."
  • XGBoost Regression: "Treats forecasting as a supervised regression problem using engineered lag and date features. Very powerful for non-linear patterns."
  • Prophet: "Facebook's open-source tool, handles seasonality and holidays automatically. Best for business-friendly outputs."

Key line to say: "I compared all models using train-test split where the last 20% of data was held out for evaluation, and selected the model with lowest MAPE."
04 Evaluation Metrics — Explained Simply +
  • MAE (Mean Absolute Error): "On average, my forecast was off by X units. Easy to understand for business people."
  • RMSE (Root Mean Squared Error): "Penalizes large errors more — important when big misses are costly (e.g. over-stocking)."
  • MAPE (Mean Abs. % Error): "My model's predictions were within X% of the actual values. Scale-independent — works across different products."
  • R² Score: "Tells how much variance in sales my model explains. 0.85 means 85% of the pattern is captured."

Golden line: "A MAPE under 10% is considered excellent for demand forecasting in practice."
05 Business Value & Real-World Impact +
Always end with business impact — this impresses everyone:
  • "Accurate demand forecasting reduces inventory costs by 20–30% (a real industry benchmark)."
  • "Prevents stockouts — which lead to lost sales — and overstocking — which ties up capital."
  • "Supply chain teams can use forecasts to plan procurement 6–12 months ahead."
  • "Marketing can align campaigns with predicted demand peaks."
  • "Finance can improve budget accuracy and cash flow projections."

Close with: "This model turns historical noise into a navigable signal — and that's the whole point of ML in business."
06 Expected Interview / Viva Questions +
  • Q: Why time-series instead of normal regression?
    A: "Normal regression assumes independent observations. Sales data has temporal dependency — past values influence future ones. Time-series models respect that structure."
  • Q: How did you handle seasonality?
    A: "Used seasonal decomposition (STL), SARIMA's seasonal component, or engineered month/quarter dummies for ML models."
  • Q: What's the difference between ARIMA and Prophet?
    A: "ARIMA is statistical, needs manual parameter tuning and stationarity. Prophet is automated, handles multiple seasonalities and holidays, more business-friendly."
  • Q: How do you validate a time-series model?
    A: "Walk-forward (rolling) cross-validation — never shuffle split, as that leaks future data into training."
  • Q: What would you improve?
    A: "Add external regressors like weather, holidays, marketing spend. Use LSTM neural networks for long-range patterns. Implement real-time retraining pipeline."
